1. Introduction: Explanatory Underdetermination
The PM operates on the axiom of informational locality: IP = f(\text{Substrate Activity}). This paper identifies specific domains where this function is subject to severe explanatory strain. The TFM is proposed not as a total replacement for the PM, but as a motivated experimental fork required to address structured information that persists under conditions of substrate compromise.
2. Stress Points in the Production Model
The following phenomena place pressure on the PM, suggesting that the relationship between substrate complexity and informational output may be inverse in specific contexts.

While residual-network and transient-synchronization hypotheses remain live competitors, TL remains underdetermined by current models of structural decay.  * Veridical NDEs: Acquisition of verifiable data during profound cerebral compromise. We acknowledge the ongoing debate regarding temporal attribution and memory formation; however, the persistence of structured experience during periods of documented cortical silence remains an anomalous pressure point for localized production theories.
