## 1. Abstract
The Transmission/Filter Model (TFM) explains biological anomalies where informational order (O_inf) remains stable or increases despite severe substrate (neurological) degradation (e.g., terminal lucidity, veridical NDE reports).  

TFM hypothesizes that biological substrates act as **high-impedance filters** for a non-local informational field originating from the Primordial Informational Field (PIF), rather than generating consciousness locally.

## 2. Inversion of the Production Model
The standard Production Model (PM) predicts:

↓ substrate integrity (S_int) → ↓ informational order (O_inf)

TFM identifies **Discontinuity Events** (e.g., terminal lucidity, high-fidelity recall during hypoxia/trauma) where:

↓ S_int → ↑ O_inf (measured via coherence proxies)

This inversion implies the substrate modulates rather than produces informational content.

## 3. Substrate Impedance and Coherence
In the Ware Constant framework (W ≈ 0.08), coherence is modulated by screening:

S(ρ) = 1 / (1 + (ρ / ρ_crit)^n)   (n ≈ 2–4)

In biological systems:
- High metabolic/neural activity → high local ρ → low S(ρ) → strong filtering (ego-bound, local consciousness)  
- Substrate degradation (hypoxia, trauma, inhibition) → reduced ρ → increased S(ρ) → reduced filtering → greater access to non-local PIF coherence

This produces the observed paradox: degraded substrate → clearer / more veridical informational retrieval.

## 5. Summary
TFM posits a **substrate-independent informational signal** modulated by neural impedance. When degradation reduces filtering, non-local PIF coherence becomes accessible, explaining terminal lucidity and related anomalies without invoking local production of consciousness.
